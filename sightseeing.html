Sightseeing

You already know about many of the City's attractions; you can even find a few by merely looking up! Read up on a few of our favorite lesser-known sighteeing attractions.

Cathedral of Saint John the Divine

The Cathedral of St. John the Divine, officially the Cathedral Church of Saint John: The Great Divine in the City and Diocese of New York, is the cathedral of the Episcopal Diocese of New York. Upon walking in, you will escape the hustle and bustle of the street. The style and design of the cathedral offers a breathtaking experience. The Cathedral is open for prayer Monday-Sunday, 7:30 am to 6:00 pm and for guided tours Monday-Saturday. Click here for more information.

City Hall Station

City Hall Station was closed in 1904 when platforms along the line were being lengthened to accommodate longer trains. This station transports you back in time, complete with a large chandelier and glass tiles. Click here here for more information.

Gracie Mansion

The home was designated a New York City Landmark on September 20, 1966 and after an extensive renovation in 2015, scheduled tours are available every Tuesday. Gracie Mansion is the official residence of the Mayor of New York City. Click here for more information.
