Restaurants

With over 24,000 places to eat in the Big Apple, deciding where to eat can be mind boggling. Check out our recommendations below.

TsuroTonTan

Craving Japanese food? Although there are too many good japanese restaurants to name, TsuroTonTan in Union Square has become a hit with locals and visitors alike since opening its door in 2016. An outpost of the original location in Japan, this eatery is most know for serving udon bowls, but it also serves sushi and japanese appetizers. The sizzling sukiayki udon bowl and the fried chicken wings are popular dishes. For more information, click here.

The Smith

If you are looking for solid, new American food, The Smith will hit the spot. There's a few locations in the city and all deliver quality food with great service. The Smith is especially popular during the weekend brunch as it's one of the few spots that takes reservations. Some of the popular brunch options are their BLTE sandwich on a croissant roll, huevos rancheros and waffles. Be sure to make a reservation here the next time you are in the Big Apple. Click here to learn more.

Javelina

Looking for Tex-Mex in NYC? Look no further as Javelina will hit the spot. Native Texans rave that the queso here is the real deal. Their grilled shrimp tacos taste like they are straight from the gulf. Located in Gramercy Park, this restaurant will be a nice departure from the hustle and bustle of the touristy areas. To experience some authentic Tex-Mex, click here.

PJ Clarkes

For some classic American pub, PJ Clarkes will have all the traditional go to bites. Some of the popular items are the burgers, fries and lobster roll. There's three locations in the city and if you find yourself visiting One World Trade Center, grab a bite at the PJ Clarkes located in World Financial Center to over look the Hudson river. Click here for more information.
